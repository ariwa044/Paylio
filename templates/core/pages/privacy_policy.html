Privacy Policy

Comprehensive Data Protection and Privacy Practices

Effective Date: January 1, 2026 | Last Updated: January 15, 2026
This Privacy Policy describes how Paylio International Holdings Corporation collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information in connection with our Services.

Introduction and Scope

Paylio International Holdings Corporation, together with its subsidiaries, affiliates, and associated entities (collectively referred to herein as "Paylio," "the Company," "we," "us," or "our"), is unequivocally committed to safeguarding the privacy and confidentiality of the personal information entrusted to us by our valued clientele, prospective customers, website visitors, and all individuals who interact with our products, services, and digital platforms.

This Privacy Policy has been meticulously crafted to provide you with comprehensive, transparent, and readily comprehensible information regarding our data collection practices, the purposes for which your information is utilized, the circumstances under which such information may be disclosed to third parties, and the mechanisms through which you may exercise your rights concerning your personal data.

By accessing, browsing, or utilizing any Paylio service, application, website, or platform, you expressly acknowledge that you have carefully reviewed and fully comprehend the terms set forth in this Privacy Policy, and you hereby consent to the collection, processing, and utilization of your personal information in accordance with the practices described herein.

1. Categories of Personal Information Collected

In the course of providing our comprehensive suite of financial services, we collect, process, and maintain various categories of personal information from and about you. The specific types of information gathered may include, but are not necessarily limited to, the following:

1.1 Identity Information

This encompasses information that serves to uniquely identify you as an individual, including:

  • Full legal name (including any former names, aliases, or variations thereof)
  • Date of birth and age
  • Gender and preferred pronouns
  • Nationality and citizenship status
  • Government-issued identification numbers (passport, driver's license, national ID card)
  • Taxpayer identification numbers (SSN, TIN, EIN, as applicable)
  • Photographic images and biometric identifiers
  • Signature specimens (physical and electronic)
1.2 Contact Information

Information that enables us to communicate with you and verify your location:

  • Residential address, mailing address, and billing address
  • Primary and secondary telephone numbers (mobile and landline)
  • Email addresses (personal and business)
  • Social media handles and identifiers (when voluntarily provided)
  • Emergency contact information
1.3 Financial Information

Information pertaining to your financial status, accounts, and transaction history:

  • Bank account details (account numbers, routing numbers, institution names)
  • Payment card information (card numbers, expiration dates, security codes)
  • Income and employment information
  • Source of funds and source of wealth documentation
  • Credit history and credit scores (as permitted by applicable law)
  • Investment portfolio information
  • Tax residency and reporting information
  • Transaction history, frequency, and patterns
1.4 Technical Information

Information automatically collected when you access our digital platforms:

  • Internet Protocol (IP) address and geolocation data
  • Device type, model, and operating system
  • Browser type, version, and language preferences
  • Screen resolution and color depth
  • Time zone settings and local time
  • Cookies, pixel tags, and similar tracking technologies
  • Referring URLs and navigation paths
  • Session duration and page interaction metrics
1.5 Sensitive Personal Information

In certain circumstances and where required by regulation, we may collect:

  • Biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition patterns, voice prints)
  • Political exposure status (for PEP screening purposes)
  • Criminal history information (where legally required for financial services)
  • Health information (only when directly relevant to your service requirements)

2. Methods of Information Collection

We employ various methodologies to collect personal information, including:

2.1 Direct Collection

Information you voluntarily provide during account registration, identity verification, customer support interactions, survey participation, and correspondence with our personnel.

2.2 Automated Collection

Information gathered automatically through cookies, web beacons, analytics tools, and similar technologies when you access our websites, mobile applications, or other digital platforms.

2.3 Third-Party Sources

Information obtained from external sources including credit bureaus, identity verification services, fraud prevention databases, public records, sanctions lists, and our business partners.

3. Purposes for Processing Personal Information

We process your personal information for the following lawful purposes:

3.1 Service Provision and Contract Performance
  • Processing account applications and maintaining account records
  • Executing financial transactions and processing payments
  • Providing customer support and responding to inquiries
  • Delivering notifications, statements, and service communications
3.2 Legal and Regulatory Compliance
  • Conducting Know Your Customer (KYC) and Customer Due Diligence (CDD) procedures
  • Screening against sanctions lists, PEP databases, and adverse media sources
  • Filing required regulatory reports (SARs, CTRs, FATCA, CRS)
  • Responding to lawful requests from government authorities and law enforcement
  • Maintaining records as required by applicable record retention requirements
3.3 Fraud Prevention and Security
  • Detecting, investigating, and preventing fraudulent or suspicious activities
  • Verifying user identity and authenticating access attempts
  • Protecting against unauthorized access, data breaches, and cyber threats
  • Enforcing our Terms of Service and other contractual agreements
3.4 Legitimate Business Interests
  • Analyzing service usage patterns to improve our offerings
  • Conducting research and development activities
  • Managing business operations and internal administration
  • Exercising and defending legal claims
3.5 Marketing and Communications (with consent)
  • Sending promotional offers, newsletters, and marketing communications
  • Personalizing advertising and content recommendations
  • Conducting customer satisfaction surveys

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your personal information with the following categories of recipients:

4.1 Affiliated Entities

Your information may be shared among Paylio subsidiaries, parent companies, and affiliated entities for purposes consistent with this Privacy Policy, including consolidated risk management, regulatory compliance, and service delivery coordination.

4.2 Service Providers

We engage third-party service providers who perform functions on our behalf, including but not limited to:

  • Cloud infrastructure and data hosting providers
  • Payment processing and settlement networks
  • Identity verification and fraud prevention services
  • Customer relationship management platforms
  • Analytics and business intelligence providers
  • Legal, accounting, and professional advisory firms

All service providers are contractually bound to maintain the confidentiality of your information and are prohibited from utilizing it for any purpose other than providing the contracted services.

4.3 Financial Institution Partners

To facilitate transactions, we share information with correspondent banks, payment networks, and other financial institutions involved in processing your transactions.

4.4 Regulatory Authorities and Law Enforcement

We disclose information to government agencies, regulators, courts, and law enforcement entities when required by law, subpoena, court order, or other legal process, or when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.

4.5 Business Transactions

In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, bankruptcy, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of the transaction, subject to standard confidentiality arrangements.

5. International Data Transfers

As a global financial services provider, we transfer personal information across international borders. When transferring data outside your country of residence, we implement appropriate safeguards including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses approved by relevant regulatory authorities
  • Binding Corporate Rules for intra-group transfers
  • Certification under recognized data protection frameworks
  • Reliance on adequacy decisions issued by data protection authorities

6. Data Security Measures

We implement comprehensive technical, administrative, and physical security measures to protect your personal information, including:

  • Advanced encryption protocols (AES-256, TLS 1.3) for data in transit and at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication and biometric access controls
  • Continuous intrusion detection and prevention systems
  • Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments
  • Employee security awareness training and background checks
  • Incident response and disaster recovery procedures
  • Physical security measures at data center facilities

While we strive to protect your information using commercially reasonable means, no method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Specific retention periods may vary based on:

  • Regulatory requirements (financial records typically retained for 5-7 years)
  • Statute of limitations for potential legal claims
  • Legitimate business purposes
  • Your continued use of our Services

Upon expiration of applicable retention periods, personal information is securely deleted or anonymized in accordance with our data destruction policies.

8. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

8.1 Right to Access

You may request confirmation of whether we process your personal information and obtain a copy of such data in a commonly used electronic format.

8.2 Right to Rectification

You may request correction of inaccurate personal information or completion of incomplete data.

8.3 Right to Erasure

You may request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements and legitimate business interests.

8.4 Right to Restriction

You may request limitation of processing in certain circumstances, such as when accuracy is contested or processing is unlawful.

8.5 Right to Data Portability

You may request transfer of your personal information to another service provider in a structured, machine-readable format.

8.6 Right to Object

You may object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.

8.7 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

8.8 Right to Lodge a Complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in your jurisdiction.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer using the contact information provided below.

9. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyze usage patterns, and deliver personalized content. For detailed information about our cookie practices and your choices, please refer to our Cookie Policy.

10. Children's Privacy

Our Services are not directed to individuals under the age of eighteen (18), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected information from a child, we will promptly delete such information from our records.

11. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act, including the right to know, delete, and opt-out of the sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information as defined by the CCPA.

12. European Data Protection (GDPR)

For individuals in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Switzerland, Paylio processes personal data in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation and applicable local data protection laws. Our legal bases for processing include contract performance, legal obligation, legitimate interests, and consent.

13. Updates to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or business operations. Material changes will be communicated through our website, email notification, or other appropriate means. We encourage you to review this Policy regularly.
